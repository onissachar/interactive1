• I thought that the best way to gain some insight into the kind of people that frequent the block would be to listen to what they had to say, so that is what I did. I spent some time hanging around different areas of of the block and eavesdropping on the conversations of customers and passersby.

  • You may think that after somebody spends this much time on a block they must know it off by heart but this is almost impossible for somebody with a block like mine. The landscape of the block is constantly changing because of the influx of flyers coming in each day.

  • Listening to a place is a great way to interact with it and the loudness of the block is one of my favourite things about it. Its loudness is what makes it seem weirdly warm and welcoming.
